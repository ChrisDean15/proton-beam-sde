##### MF33: COVARIANCES OF NEUTRON CROSS SECTIONS                 1925 1451  160
                                                                  1925 1451  161
In MF33, we store the covariances of most, but not all, reaction  1925 1451  162
channels that are given in MF3. All covariances are generated     1925 1451  163
with the TASMAN code [kon19], which generates TALYS runs using    1925 1451  164
a Monte Carlo parameter method.                                   1925 1451  165
For each MT-number, the information consists of:                  1925 1451  166
1. Covariances for MTa, Ea and MTa, Ea (variances)                1925 1451  167
2. Covariances for MTa, Ea and MTa, Eb (correlations for the      1925 1451  168
   same reaction channel between different energies)              1925 1451  169
3. Covariances for MTa, Ea and MTb, Ea (correlations between      1925 1451  170
   different reaction channels for the same energy)               1925 1451  171
4. Covariances for MTa, Ea and MTb, Eb (correlations between      1925 1451  172
   different reaction channels for different energies)            1925 1451  173
Classes 1 and 2 are covered by NI-type subsubsections with        1925 1451  174
flag LB=5, followed by a LB=8 section which contains              1925 1451  175
the cross section variances multiplied by 1.e-3. Next, classes 3  1925 1451  176
and 4 are covered by flag LB=6 to account for the different       1925 1451  177
energy grids that two correlating MT-sections may have. Since     1925 1451  178
the total correlation matrix is fully symmetric, we only need to  1925 1451  179
include the correlation elements between different MT-numbers     1925 1451  180
once, and we do that in the subsection with the lowest of the     1925 1451  181
two MT-numbers. We include covariance information for all the     1925 1451  182
total cross sections, the lumped partial cross sections and the   1925 1451  183
individual inelastic cross sections per level (MT51,...). We do   1925 1451  184
not include covariances for MT600 and higher MT-numbers. For the  1925 1451  185
most important channels, we include cross-channel correlations.   1925 1451  186
